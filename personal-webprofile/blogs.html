Difference Between Microservices and Monolithic Architecture

Monolithic Architecture
Microservices Architecture

A monolithic architecture is a single, unified codebase where all components and functions of an application are tightly coupled and run as one service. In contrast, microservices architecture breaks down the application into smaller, independent services that communicate over APIs. Microservices offer better scalability, flexibility, and fault isolation, while monolithic systems are simpler to develop and deploy but can become difficult to maintain as they grow.

Aspect Monolithic Microservices
Architecture Single codebase, tightly coupled Multiple independent services
Scalability Limited, scales as a whole Highly scalable, per service
Deployment Deployed as one unit Deployed independently
Fault Isolation Low High
Technology Stack Usually one Can vary per service
